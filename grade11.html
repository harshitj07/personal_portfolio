Housing Insecurity Project

For our major project this year, we were given a significant amount of time to formulate our ideas and gradually build toward a final product. We began by completing several modules focused on researching governance in our region, building empathy, and determining our primary approach to addressing the housing crisis. Once this foundation was established, we pitched our idea to others and received valuable feedback, which we used to iterate on our design and form a solid basis for our solution. The remainder of the project followed a self-paced structure, during which we designed a prototype and created sketches illustrating how the model would function in the real world. The project concluded with a presentation and proposal that we shared during a showcase at the end of the semester. Through this experience, we gained a stronger understanding of how real-world projects operate, particularly in terms of organization and managing our work at a comfortable pace to meet deadlines.
